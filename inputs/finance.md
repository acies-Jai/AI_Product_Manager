# Finance Team — Context

## Team
CFO (Ananya Rao) + Finance Business Partner for Product (Saurabh Jain).

## Unit Economics (per order, Q1 FY26)
- Avg Order Value (AOV): ₹520
- Contribution Margin: ₹18/order (3.5%) — target: ₹52/order (10%)
- Delivery cost: ₹38/order
- Dark store variable cost (picking, packaging): ₹22/order
- Discount / subsidy burn: ₹44/order (needs reduction)
- Payment gateway cost: ₹6/order

## P&L Levers Flagged
1. Subsidy burn is the biggest drag — discount-led acquisition inflates CAC and hurts CM
2. Payment failures cost ₹12Cr/month in failed transaction re-attempts + CS handling
3. Refund processing costs ₹8/ticket in agent time — 2.1M tickets/month = ₹16.8Cr/month
4. Checkout latency directly correlates with abandonment — 1s improvement = est. ₹3.2Cr/month revenue recovery

## Investment Priorities (from board)
1. Reduce refund/CS cost through automation — highest ROI
2. Improve OCR — direct GMV impact
3. Reduce payment failure rate — immediate P&L impact
4. Reduce CAC through better activation/referral — growth efficiency

## Budget Available for Product Initiatives (H1 FY26)
- Engineering capacity investment: ₹6.5 Cr (headcount already hired)
- Tooling / vendor: ₹80L available
- Any initiative displacing a paid vendor needs PM sign-off + Finance approval

## Constraints
- No new headcount until contribution margin hits 6%
- Discount budgets frozen — growth must come from product-led improvements, not subsidy
